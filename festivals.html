Festivals of India

A land where every season brings a celebration.

🪔

Diwali

October / November

The Festival of Lights marks the victory of light over darkness. Homes are lit with diyas and lanterns, streets glow with rangoli, and families gather for feasts and fireworks across the country.

🎨

Holi

March

The Festival of Colors welcomes spring with an explosion of powder and water. Streets, temples, and courtyards fill with laughter as people of all ages throw color at one another in joyful celebration.

🙏

Durga Puja

September / October

West Bengal transforms during this ten-day celebration of Goddess Durga. Elaborate pandals, artistic idols, and vibrant processions fill Kolkata and beyond, culminating in an emotional immersion ceremony.

🌙

Eid al-Fitr

Varies (lunar calendar)

Marking the end of the holy month of Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr is a day of prayer, gratitude, and feasting. Families gather in new clothes, share sweets like sheer khurma, and exchange gifts with loved ones.

🎄

Christmas

December 25

Celebrated with warmth across India, Christmas brings twinkling lights, decorated trees, midnight mass, and festive markets — especially vibrant in Goa, Kerala, and the northeastern states.

☬

Guru Nanak Jayanti

November

Commemorating the birth of the first Sikh Guru, this day is marked by processions, hymn recitations, and community kitchens (langar) that serve free meals to all, regardless of faith or background.

🌾

Baisakhi

April 13/14

Marking the Punjabi New Year and the wheat harvest, Baisakhi is celebrated with energetic bhangra and giddha dances, fairs, and community feasts across Punjab's golden fields.

🐘

Ganesh Chaturthi

August / September

Elaborate clay idols of Lord Ganesha are welcomed into homes and public pandals across Maharashtra, celebrated for ten days with music and devotion before an emotional immersion in water.

🧵

Raksha Bandhan

August

Sisters tie a sacred thread, the rakhi, on their brothers' wrists as a symbol of protection and love, receiving gifts and blessings in return — a celebration of the sibling bond.
